An Orb of Conversion is a very Rare and powerful item that can turn a Rare item into an equivalent Unique item. This provides players with a chance to obtain powerful items without having to find them the traditional way.

An Orb of Conversion can drop from any mob in the game, on any difficulty.

To use an Orb of Conversion, transmute it with a Rare item. When rolling to determine the output item, keep the following in mind:

As a rule of thumb, for vanilla items you must exceed the item's required level by 8 to be able to convert into it (e.g. Thunder Stroke requires level 69, but to convert a Rare Matriarchal Javelin into a Thunder Stroke you must be at least level 77). There are some exceptions, such as Tyrael's Might, which requires level 84 to wear, but 87 to craft.

For modded items, you must meet or exceed the item's required level.

Orb of Assemblage (Coming Soon in 2.0)

Like the Orb of Conversion, the Orb of Assemblage allows players to turn Rare items into an equivalent Set item. This provides players with a chance to complete sets much easier without having to find them all the traditional way.

An Orb of Assemblage can drop from any mob in the game, on any difficulty.

To use an Orb of Assemblage, transmute it with a Rare item. When rolling to determine the output item, keep the following in mind:

As a rule of thumb, for vanilla items you must exceed the item's required level by 8 to be able to convert into it.

For modded items, you must meet or exceed the item's required level.

Orb of Infusion (Coming Soon in 2.0)

The Orb of Infusion allows players to turn white items (or Magic Jewelry) into an equivalent Rare item.

An Orb of Infusion can drop from any mob in the game, on any difficulty, and is roughly 3x as likely to be seen as Conversion/Assemblage orbs.

To use an Orb of Infusion, transmute it with a white base item or Magic jewerly (ring/amulet).

Orb of Shadows (Coming Soon)

The Orb of Shadows allows players to force items to be Ethereal.

An Orb of Shadows can drop from any mob in the game, on any difficulty, and is roughly half as likely to be seen as Conversion/Assemblage orbs.

To use an Orb of Shadows, transmute it with an item (the item must have durability).

Orb of Socketing

The Orb of Socketing is a useful tool that allows players to add a random number of sockets to an item, up to the item's max, even on Set and Unique items!

Simply transmute an Orb of Socketing with an item in the Horadric Cube.

Players can use an Orb of Socketing on items that already have sockets to re-roll the number of open sockets.

The Orb of Socketing replaces essence drops from Hell Act bosses & Key Wardens.

Orb of Corruption

Transmuting an item in the Horadric Cube with an Orb of Corruption allows you to potentially add beneficial stats to an item (weapons, armor, shields).

WARNING: DO NOT CORRUPT RUNEWORDS. Corrupt the base item first, and then craft your runeword.

WARNING: when corrupting Set ITEMS make sure you have only ONE of them in your personal stash/inventory at a time until you have finished corrupting. If you are benefitting from Set bonuses then the corruption will be added to the Set bonus and won't be visible otherwise.

How to Find Corruption Orbs

Drop chances vary by difficulty:

  • Normal: Low chance
  • Nightmare: Medium chance
  • Hell: High chance

Note: Only quest-related bosses drop the orb when they are Terrorized.

How to Use Corruption Orbs

Using the Corruption Orb is straightforward:

  1. Place the Corruption Orb and an item (Weapon, Armor or Shield) in the Horadric Cube
  2. Press the transmute button - you will hear the transmute sound, and your item will gain the CORRUPTED tag
    1. The property roll happens behind the scenes, determining the final outcome but not yet applying it
  3. Press the transmute button again
    1. If you hear the transmute sound and your orb disappears, the corruption has succeeded!
      1. The second transmute actually applies the outcome to the item. NOTE: there is a possibility that nothing is added!
    2. If you don't hear a transmute sound and you still have your orb, the corruption has failed...
      1. Note: The orb is not consumed until a property is applied - if it fails this time, try again on another item

Once something has been corrupted you cannot corrupt it again.
